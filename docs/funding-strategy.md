# Tahti ry — funding strategy

Foundation grants and donations are not optional decoration on the model — they
are required revenue. This document is the plan to secure them.

## Year-by-year funding mix

| | Subs + dist | Grants | Donations | Total revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Y1 | €13,110 (34%) | €25,000 (64%) | €800 (2%) | €38,910 |
| Y2 | €83,160 (65%) | €45,000 (35%) | €4,800 (4%) | €128,460 |
| Y3 | €262,200 (73%) | €80,000 (22%) | €16,000 (4%) | €358,200 |

Grant dependency drops over time as subscription revenue grows — by Y3 we're a
mostly subscription-supported organization with grants as supplement, not lifeline.

## Y1 — survival year, secure the founding grant

**The €25,000 target.** This accelerates Y1 capex and member growth — hardware,
legal, and outreach — not payroll survival. Operating costs balance without a
fixed director salary; the maintenance team (including the director) shares
surplus **equally** when the year is in the black (see `financial-model.md`).

### Primary target: Business Finland Tempo

Tempo grants fund early-stage Finnish companies with high-growth potential.
Nonprofits *with commercial elements* are eligible (we have subscription revenue,
so we qualify).

- **Amount:** up to €50,000
- **Decision timeline:** 8–12 weeks from application
- **Co-funding:** 50% (i.e. €25k granted requires €25k of our own committed
  cash); we use the subscription revenue ramp as our co-funding
- **Application effort:** 30–50 hours; the gap analysis and budget detail is
  the hard part
- **Pitch angle:** "open-source EU infrastructure for independent artists,
  reducing dependence on US streaming platforms"

### Backup target: Koneen Säätiö

Kone Foundation supports bold cultural and societal innovation. Strong fit for
us.

- **Amount:** typically €15k–40k for startup-stage cultural projects
- **Decision timeline:** ~3-month cycle (twice yearly)
- **Application effort:** 20–30 hours
- **Pitch angle:** "infrastructure for sustainable independent music in the
  Nordics; member-governed alternative to commercial platforms"

### Tertiary: Suomen Kulttuurirahasto

Finnish Cultural Foundation has multiple programs; the central fund and
regional funds both award smaller grants.

- **Amount:** €5k–20k typical
- **Decision timeline:** annual cycle
- **Application effort:** 15–25 hours

### Strategy

Apply to all three in Y0 (the months before formal launch). The Tempo grant is
the best fit but most competitive. Koneen as warm backup. Kulttuurirahasto as
top-up if first two come through.

**Plan for one of these to come through, not all three.** If only €15k arrives
instead of €25k, the deficit grows by €10k — manageable by deferring some legal
spend and trimming initial capex.

## Y2 — diversify the grant base

By Y2 the organization has 12 months of operating history, audited financials,
and a real platform serving real artists. Grant application surface broadens.

### Targets

- **Koneen Säätiö renewal or new program** — €20k
- **Suomen Kulttuurirahasto regional fund** — €10k
- **Helsinki city culture grants** — €5–10k (smaller but accessible)
- **Music Finland (Musex)** — if export angle developed — €5–10k
- **Aligned tech-cultural sponsorship** — Bandcamp / Mixcloud / Distrokid might
  see strategic value in cross-promotion — €10k+

**Total target Y2: €45,000.** Realistic given the operating track record.

### Creative Europe — the big bet

Creative Europe's Culture strand awards €30k–150k for cross-border European
cultural projects. Tahti ry partnering with a Berlin or Tallinn collective
fits.

- **Application:** complex, ~80 hours of work, partnership required
- **Decision:** annual cycle, ~6 months from deadline to result
- **If awarded:** likely covers a meaningful chunk of Y3 grant revenue
- **Apply by:** end of Y2 to potentially fund Y3 operations

Don't count on this for Y3 in the base model — but if it lands, it's a major
upside.

## Y3 — institutional

By Y3, established nonprofit with audited financials, member governance, and
demonstrable artist impact. Larger funders become accessible.

### Targets

- **Creative Europe** (if Y2 application succeeded) — €30–80k
- **Koneen Säätiö continuation** — €30k
- **EU Music Moves Europe** (if relaunched) — €10–30k
- **Nordic Culture Fund** — €10–30k
- **Aligned major donors** — €5–20k

**Total target Y3: €80,000.** Achievable but requires sustained application
hygiene throughout Y2 and Y3.

## Donation strategy

Donations are not the main lever (4% of total revenue at maturity), but they
matter for:

1. **Signaling** — a thriving donation base demonstrates mission resonance to
   grant officers
2. **Direct artist support** — donations explicitly tied to the grant pool
   (donor checks a box; their money flows directly into next year's grants,
   not into operations)
3. **Major-gift opportunities** — occasionally a single supporter contributes
   €500–5,000

### Channels

- **One-time donations** — Stripe checkout, standard product flow
- **Recurring donations** — monthly €5/€10/€25 tiers via Stripe subscriptions,
  separate product SKU from artist subscriptions
- **Gift-the-grant-pool checkbox** — every donation form has a checkbox: "Send
  my donation to the artist grant pool, not operations" (default checked for
  large gifts, unchecked for small ones)
- **Year-end appeal** — December campaign, "the more we receive, the more we
  give to artists in March"

## Sponsorship policy

Sponsorship complicates the nonprofit story if not handled carefully. Tahti ry
policy:

**Accept:**
- Aligned tech/cultural orgs (Bandcamp, Mixcloud, Resonate)
- Music technology companies with no direct platform competition
- Foundations and cultural institutions

**Decline:**
- DSPs (Spotify, Apple Music, Tidal) — competitive conflict
- Major labels — power imbalance with our artists
- Tobacco, alcohol, gambling, weapons (standard nonprofit exclusion)
- Anyone who wants logo placement in the player or on channel pages

**Where sponsor logos appear:**
- Annual report (yes)
- Transparency dashboard "supporters" page (yes)
- Marketing site footer (yes)
- Anywhere artists or listeners encounter (no — never)

## The honest constraint

Foundation grant officers do not write checks for vague missions. They write
checks for:

1. **Concrete impact** — "we will support 200 artists in Year 1, distributing
   €X in grants by Year 3"
2. **Measurable outcomes** — listener-hours, grants disbursed, geographic reach,
   demographics of supported artists
3. **Organizational health** — audited financials, board diversity, clear
   bylaws, transparent operations
4. **Strategic fit** — the foundation's program goals must match what we do

Build the application infrastructure early. By Y1Q3 you should have:
- A 2-page case statement
- A 1-page financial summary
- A 4-page logic model (inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes → impact)
- A list of artists who would write a letter of support
- Photos and stories from beta users
- A 3-minute video explaining what we do

Without these, you'll repeatedly write the same content from scratch and
applications will take 2× longer than necessary.
