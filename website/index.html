It's worse than just scattered.

Each platform has a structural problem you can't fix by trying harder.

SoundCloud & Mixcloud

Audio quality is capped at the listener tier, not you

You pay Pro. Your listeners still hear 128 kbps Opus or 64 kbps AAC. Your master sounds like a YouTube rip to the people you made it for. Even when you pay, your audience suffers — unless they also pay.

Spotify

Overrun by AI, no required disclosure

Documented in Music Business Worldwide: AI-generated tracks in curated playlists, "ghost artist" placeholder names. Your original work competes with infinite-supply autogenerated audio for the same algorithmic surfacing.

Bandcamp

Pays fairly — but it's a store, not a station

82–85% to artists is the best rate in commercial music. But Bandcamp is fundamentally a record store: no 24/7 channel, no live broadcasting, no real-time chat. Good at selling albums. Not what artists need for broadcasting.

WebGL visualizers. Cover art palettes. Slideshow themes.

Every channel has its own visual identity. Choose a WebGL ambient preset, set a slideshow transition style, or let cover art automatically extract a 5-color scheme that flows through your entire page — from one section in the dashboard.

Five WebGL presets

Three.js · GPU-rendered · lazy-loaded

Waveform bars, particle field, aurora borealis shader, reactive grid — or off. Each preset adapts its colors to your scheme. Gated on prefers-reduced-motion and WebGL2 support.

🎨

Auto palette from cover art

Extracted on upload · fire-and-forget

Upload a release cover. Tahti extracts a 5-color scheme — background, accent, text, muted, highlight — and applies it as CSS custom properties on your smart link page automatically. Override any color manually.

▷

Four slideshow transitions

FADE · ZOOM · PAN · BLUR_CROSS

Gallery images move between slides with your choice of transition. Interval, duration, and autoplay — all adjustable. Keyframes injected once, plays at native GPU speed.

🖌

Per-release color override

Each release can override the extracted palette with custom colors. The smart link page inherits the release color scheme — looks correct on every platform that embeds your link.

⚙

Per-archive-item presets

Each archived set can carry its own visualization preset. A dark ambient mix gets a different mood than a peak-hour techno set — individual control, not just channel-wide.

🔒

Scoped, not global

Color tokens apply to the page wrapper — not :root. They don't bleed into nav or other pages during Next.js client-side navigation.

Two ways money reaches you.

Direct from fans who support you. Plus a yearly grant from the org's surplus, weighted by engagement.
💛

Direct fan-subscriptions

0% org take

Fans subscribe directly to you for €1–€100/month. Money flows fan → Stripe → you, with a 2% operational fee covering processing, GDPR, and support. Tahti ry takes zero. Subscribers get a badge, FLAC downloads, fan-only chat.

€163k
to artists, 3yr total
🌱

Annual grant from surplus

Weighted by engagement units

After audit each year, 90% of org surplus flows to artists as grants. Your share depends on engagement units: free downloads count 1×, paid-subscriber downloads count 5×, each €1 of fan-sub revenue counts 1×. Passive listening doesn't count.

€150k
to artists, 3yr total
TOTAL OVER 3 YEARS

~€312,000 in artists' hands — 46% more than a model where the org takes a cut. Yours because of how we routed the money, not because we generated more of it.

Not a user. Not a customer.

Every member votes equally at the AGM. Founder's vote = newest member's vote.

Members can propose motions. Pricing, grant formula, storage policy, board composition — anything is up for debate.
